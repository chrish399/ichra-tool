The problem

Sound familiar?

These are the reasons most employers start looking at ICHRA.

01

Annual renewals you can't predict

Your group plan premium changes every year based on your employees' claims. One bad year and everyone pays. ICHRA gives you a fixed monthly amount you set — permanently.

02

Participation minimums blocking you

Traditional group plans require 70%+ employee participation. If yours don't enroll, you lose coverage entirely. ICHRA has zero minimums — offer benefits to any employee, any time.

03

Coverage that doesn't work across states

Managing one group plan for a multi-state team means network gaps and compliance headaches. ICHRA works identically in all 50 states — each employee picks what works in their market.

04

Taxable stipends wasting your money

A $400/month taxable stipend loses roughly 30% to FICA and income taxes before it helps anyone. The same $400 as an ICHRA allowance goes to the employee 100% intact, tax-free.

Key benefits

Why employers are switching

01

Predictable, fixed costs

Set a fixed monthly allowance per employee. No more surprise premium increases based on claims experience.

02

Employee choice and flexibility

Employees choose from dozens of marketplace plans that fit their doctors, needs, and budget — not one carrier's limited network.

03

Fully ACA compliant

ICHRA is an official IRS regulation (2020) satisfying ACA employer mandate requirements when properly structured.

04

No minimums — zero participation requirements

Unlike group plans, ICHRA has no minimum contribution amounts and no employee participation thresholds.

05

Works in all 50 states

Multi-state teams are fully covered. Each employee participates in their local ACA marketplace — no network gaps.

06

Simplified administration

No carrier negotiations, no network management. Approve monthly reimbursements through a simple admin platform.
